Classification Criteria & Methodology

How we determine B40, M40, and T20 categories

Income Thresholds

Node 1: Income Classification (B40 / M40 / T20)
The first split in the tree determines the base national category using strict monthly household income thresholds (B40 < RM 4,850, M40 up to RM 10,970).
Node 2: Economic Strain (Per Capita & Dependents)
Within the income group, the tree evaluates the number of dependents and per capita income. For example, a B40 household with ≥ 5 dependents or per capita income ≤ RM 500 is immediately routed to the highest priority branch.
Node 3: Household Vulnerability (OKU & Elderly)
The final branch checks for the presence of disabled (OKU) or elderly (60+) members. Households meeting these criteria are elevated to a higher priority branch, ensuring vulnerable groups are not left behind.

Final Priority Outcomes

Very High / High

Immediate Aid

B40 households facing severe economic strain or containing vulnerable members.

Medium / Low

Targeted Support

Stable B40 households, or M40 households experiencing specific vulnerabilities.

Not Eligible

Stable Status

Financially stable households (M40/T20) without extraordinary compounding factors.

Our Transparency Commitment

All classifications are made using objective, data-driven criteria applied equally to all households. There is no subjective bias in the automated classification system. Every decision can be explained and reviewed upon request.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about welfare classification and decisions

How is my household classified?
Households are automatically classified into B40, M40, or T20 categories based on monthly household income. B40 (Bottom 40%): Below RM 4,850/month. M40 (Middle 40%): RM 4,851 - RM 10,970/month. T20 (Top 20%): Above RM 10,971/month.
What factors determine aid eligibility?
Eligibility is determined by a Decision Tree algorithm that evaluates your Classification Category, Dependents, Per Capita income, and the presence of vulnerable members (OKU/Elderly) to output a Priority Level.
How does the Decision Tree work?
The algorithm first splits households by income (B40, M40, T20). It then branches out based on economic strain (e.g., if a B40 household has per capita income ≤ RM 500). Finally, it checks for vulnerable members. This multi-step process ensures a fair and holistic priority score rather than relying on a single metric.
Can I appeal a classification decision?
Yes. If you believe there is a data error, you may file an appeal through the admin office.
How often are classifications updated?
The automated classification engine sweeps the database weekly to update states based on newly imported or adjusted demographic data.
What data is used for classification?
We securely use resident-provided demographic logs spanning age, dependents, and stated monthly income strictly for village-level assessments.

Still have questions?

Contact the village administration office for personalized assistance or to request a detailed review of your household classification.
